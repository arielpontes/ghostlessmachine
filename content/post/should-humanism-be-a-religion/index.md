All around the world religious institutions have special legal status. In some places they are simply tax exempt, in some they are even financed by the state, who pays to build and renovate their churches and the salaries of the clergy, sometimes they have a place in public schools and almost always have their services recognized as of ultimate importance for the citizens and a place available for their chaplains in public institutions such as hospitals, the army, prisons, etc.
