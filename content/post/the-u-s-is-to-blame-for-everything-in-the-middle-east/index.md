I know very little about world history, U.S. foreign policy or the social-economic background of the Middle East and have therefore hesitated to write on this topic. However, since it has recently become very popular for people who know as much as me (if not less) to be nauseatingly loud about it, I figured it was time to join the party as well.
You must have heard this discourse at least once, and may even have spouted it yourself at some point: “the U.S. is the one to blame for this mess in the Middle East! If it wasn't for the U.S. and their economic interests in the region, all this war wouldn't happen! All they want is oil! Don't you think it's a strange coincidence that they never intervene in countries that don't have oil? And not only do they profit from the oil itself, but also from maintaining the war and selling weapons!”
Of course, economic interests play an important role in international politics, but if you assign so much importance to it that it overshadows all the other, there's no subtler way of saying it, but you may be fallen victim to a conspiracy theory.
