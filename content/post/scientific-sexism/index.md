The other day at work a guy made a comment about women being bad drivers. He went on and defended himself saying this doesn't imply he's sexist, and criticized women who get irritated by this type of comment. “There are differences between men and women, this is a simple fact of nature, it's irrational to deny it. It doesn't mean men are overall better than women. As much as there are things men are better at, there are things women are better at”, he said. I don't deny that there may be cognitive differences between men and women. But this is a very dangerous statement, often accompanied by very dangerous and unwarranted assumptions, and one must be very careful when making it. Unfortunately, many are so uninformed about our sexist history that they don't realize this danger. It just seems like a reasonable, moderate, harmless claim. It is not.

# Nature vs. nurture

Everything we know about reproduction, Darwinism and evolutionary pressures leads to the inevitable conclusion that males and females will, to some extent, evolve different behavioral tendencies. Granted. But unlike most animals, humans, through complex communication and the ability to accumulate knowledge and transmit customs, gave rise to a myriad of different cultures and societal structures. One of our most distinctive characteristics compared to other species is our flexibility and our capacity to adapt in response to our surroundings, not as a species through evolution, but as individuals through socialization and enculturation.
This makes it very hard to argue, given any specific behavioral difference between sexes, that it is natural and not cultural, even though we know that some tendencies must indeed be intrinsic.
